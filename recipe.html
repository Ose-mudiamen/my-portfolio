Egusi Soup

Intro

Cooking time: 45 min | Serves 4

Egusi soup is a rich, savory West African delicacy made with ground melon seeds, palm oil, meat, and leafy greens. It is highly popular in Nigerian cuisine and is traditionally enjoyed with 'swallows' like pounded yam, eba, or fufu

    Ingredients

  • Egusi seeds: 4 cups (ground/blended)
  • Proteins: 1 to 2 lbs assorted meat (beef, goat, or tripe) and 1 lb fish (smoked, dry, or fresh fish/shrimps)
  • Vegetables: 3 cups chopped spinach, kale, or fluted pumpkin leaves (Ugu)
  • Base: ½ cup red palm oil
  • Pepper & Aromatics: 1 blended mix of habanero/scotch bonnet peppers, 1 onion, and 1 bell pepper
  • Seasoning: 3 tablespoons ground crayfish, 2-3 seasoning cubes, and salt to taste
  • Liquid: 2 to 3 cups

    Instructions

  1. Prepare the Proteins and Egusi Paste
    • Season your meats with salt, onion, and spices, and boil them until tender. Reserve the leftover water/stock.
    • In a separate bowl, mix your ground egusi seeds with a few tablespoons of water to form a thick paste. (You can also blend it with an onion for extra flavor).
  2. Fry the Base
    • Heat the red palm oil in a large pot over medium heat for about 3 minutes.
    • Add chopped onions and fry until translucent, then stir in your blended pepper mix and let it cook for 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Add the Egusi
    • Scoop the egusi paste into the boiling pepper and oil mixture in small clumps. Do not stir immediately, as this allows the egusi to form the signature large, flavorful lumps.
    • Cover and let it simmer on low heat for about 10 minutes.
  4. Combine and Simmer
    • Gently stir the egusi curds. Slowly add in your cooked meats, dried fish, crayfish, and your reserved meat stock.
    • Season with your seasoning cubes and salt, and let the soup simmer for another 10 to 15 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  5. Add the Vegetables
    • Stir in your chopped greens and let them cook for just 3-5 minutes until wilted but still bright green.
    • Remove from heat and serve hot!

The secret is frying the paste until the oil separates.
